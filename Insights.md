# Key Insights

## Overall Flight Volume
* **Total Volume:** The analysis covers nearly **2 million** commercial flights operating across major US airports in 2015.
* **Delays:** Approximately **58,000 flights** were delayed (~3% of total flights).
* **Cancellations:** Around **29,000 flights** were cancelled.

---

## Busiest Airports
Atlanta recorded the highest flight traffic, handling approximately **400,000 flights** (nearly twice the volume of Chicago). 

The top three busiest airports were:
1. Atlanta
2. Chicago
3. Dallas–Fort Worth

> **Note:** High-traffic airports tend to experience a greater number of operational disruptions due to sheer flight volume complexity.

---

## Airline Delay Performance
* **Highest Delay Rates:** Southwest (WN) and United (UA) exhibited the highest delay rates among the analyzed airlines.
* **Lowest Delay Rates:** ExpressJet (EV) and Alaska Airlines (AS) showed comparatively lower delay rates.
* **Network Impact:** Airlines operating larger networks often experience more delays due to the operational complexity associated with higher flight volumes.

---

## Cancellation Analysis
* **Weather-Related:** The leading cause of disruptions, accounting for approximately **16,000** cancelled flights.
* **Carrier-Related:** Airline and carrier issues represented the second most common reason, contributing around **8,000** cancellations.
* **Security-Related:** Had a minimal impact on overall operations.

---

## Delay Trends by Day of Week
* **Friday:** Experienced the **highest** delay rates, likely driven by increased end-of-week travel demand.
* **Tuesday:** Recorded the **lowest** delay rate, making it the most reliable day for travelers seeking to minimize disruptions.
* **Weekends:** Contrary to common assumptions, weekends did not exhibit the highest delay frequencies.

---

## Key Takeaways
* **Strategic Travel Windows:** To minimize the risk of disruptions, travelers should consider booking flights on **Tuesdays**, which consistently demonstrate the lowest delay rates, while avoiding peak end-of-week demand on **Fridays**.
* **Primary Driver of Cancellations:** Operational data reveals that severe **weather conditions** disrupt flight schedules far more severely than internal airline or carrier operational issues, making real-time weather monitoring a crucial factor in travel planning.
